Maya Angelou

The Life of Maya Angelou

  • April 4, 1928- Born Marguerite Annie Johnson
  • 1972- Published 'New York Times' best-seller 'I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings'
  • 1973- Obtained Tony Award nomination For her role in the Broadway play 'Look Away'
  • 1991- Given Langston Hughes Medal which for African-American writers who explore their cultural heritage
  • 1993- Received first Grammy for poem 'On the Pulse of Morning'
  • 1994- Received Springarn Medal by the National Associaton for the Advancement of Colored People
  • 2000-Awarded National Medal of Arts
  • 2010- Awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2013- Given the Literarian Award from the National Book Foundation
  • May 28, 2014- Passed away at the age of 86
